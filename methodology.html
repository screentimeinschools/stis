How We Work

Methodology

This tracker aims to be the most accurate and current public resource on school phone and screen time policy in the United States. Here's how the data is collected, categorized, and updated.

Data Sources

State laws

Sourced directly from state legislature websites, governor signing announcements, and LegiScan. Every state entry links to the primary bill text or official state DOE page. We do not rely on press releases or secondary sources for the legislative record.

District actions

Sourced from local school board minutes, district press releases, and verified local news coverage. Districts are included when a formal board vote or written policy is on record — informal statements or verbal commitments are not included.

Community advocacy

Parent groups and grassroots campaigns are included when they have demonstrated a formal, organized presence — a public meeting, a filed petition, or documented media coverage. The advocacy category is not a judgment of the group's position, only a record of their existence and activity.

Policy Categories

  • Bell-to-bell ban — state law prohibits personal phone use from first bell to last bell, covering all K–12 students.
  • Class restriction — phones must be stored during instructional time but may be used during passing periods or lunch.
  • District policies — no statewide law, but significant district-level action is documented here.
  • Guidance only — the state has issued non-binding guidance encouraging districts to restrict phones, but no law has passed.
  • Social media limits — law targets social media access specifically, rather than phones broadly.
  • Pending — legislation has been introduced and is actively moving through the legislature but has not yet been enacted.
  • No law — no statewide policy exists; individual districts set their own rules.

News Categorization

Every article is automatically tagged based on keywords in its headline and summary:

  • 📱 Phone Ban — article primarily covers personal phone bans, phone-free policies, Yondr pouches, or phone storage requirements.
  • 💻 Screen Time — article primarily covers school-issued device limits, Chromebook policies, classroom screen time caps, or ed-tech restrictions.
  • 📰 Other — article was pulled by the news scraper but is not directly about phone or screen time policy. These appear in the All News feed but not on the homepage Top 10.

News Collection

News articles are collected automatically each day via a GitHub Actions workflow that searches Google News RSS feeds across all 50 states using targeted queries — phrases like "[State] school cell phone ban," "[State] school screen time," and "[State] classroom phone policy." Articles are scored for relevance using a keyword weighting system and filtered to remove items with low scores.

The pipeline requires no API key and runs entirely on open data. Source code is publicly available in the GitHub repository.

Update Frequency

News articles update daily via automated GitHub Actions. State policy data is reviewed and updated manually when new laws are enacted, when significant district actions are reported, or when readers submit corrections. The "Updated daily" indicator in the site header refers to the news feed only.

Corrections

If you spot an error — a misclassified law, a missing district action, an outdated status, or an article that shouldn't be tagged the way it is — please reach out via Substack. We take accuracy seriously and will correct errors promptly.

All primary sources are linked directly from each state's entry in the tracker. If a source link is broken or incorrect, that's the highest-priority correction we make.
