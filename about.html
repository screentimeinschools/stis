About This Project

Screen Time in Schools

An independent, nonpartisan tracker documenting every state-level law, board resolution, and advocacy effort aimed at reducing student screen time and banning personal phones in K–12 classrooms across the United States.

Who made this?

This site was built and is maintained by Danie Ulacco, a New Hampshire educator with over a decade of experience in elementary and middle school classrooms. After watching the impact of devices on student focus, social development, and mental health firsthand, she set out to create the most comprehensive public resource on school phone and screen time policy in America.

Why it matters

Researchers, journalists, policymakers, and parents increasingly need a reliable, up-to-date reference for what's actually happening across the country. State laws are changing rapidly — and the details matter. This tracker surfaces the legislation, the district actions, the community advocacy, and the research behind the movement.

Every entry links directly to primary sources: bill text on state legislature websites, official DOE guidance pages, and governor signing announcements. No paywalls, no aggregators — just the original documents.

Is this affiliated with any organization?

No. This is an independent project. It is not funded by, affiliated with, or endorsed by any advocacy group, technology company, school district, or government agency.
